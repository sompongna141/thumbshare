# Product

## Register

product

## Users

YouTubers, channel managers, editors, and small creator agencies who are packaging a video and need strong thumbnail directions before design or generation starts. They are usually working under time pressure and want a fast, decisive brief they can act on immediately.

## Product Purpose

ThumbSnare turns a rough video idea into six differentiated thumbnail directions with image prompts, face-expression guidance, overlay copy, color rationale, and an A/B plan. It exists to help creators make better packaging decisions faster, not to be a generic AI form. Success means a user can go from title to shortlist-worthy concepts in under five minutes.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, cinematic, creator-native. The interface should feel confident, fast, and conversion-aware, like a thumbnail strategy desk rather than a toy prompt generator.

## Anti-references

Generic AI chat layouts, bland single-page forms, decorative SaaS gradients, stock admin dashboards, and anything that feels like raw prompt dumping. It should not look like a template generator or a debug console.

## Design Principles

- Guide the user through packaging decisions in a clear sequence.
- Keep the strategic signal visible so every field feels connected to click-through performance.
- Make review moments feel decisive, not bureaucratic.
- Keep advanced controls available but secondary to the main flow.
- Treat outputs like editorial concepts worth comparing and shortlisting.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard access across the full flow. Respect reduced-motion preferences, avoid color-only status cues, and keep step progress, validation, and action states legible on both desktop and mobile.
